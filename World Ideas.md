# World Ideas

The shared idea backlog for Elastic Space, with a section per contributor — Claude's ideas and
James's ideas. Numbering is global across sections so any world can be cited as "idea #N".
Future sessions should update statuses here rather than re-pitching from scratch.

Began life as `Claude's Ideas.md`: fifty world pitches generated 2026-07-10 by claude-fable in
session with James, plus the method, the B-sides that got cut, and build notes for the first
selections. Renamed and restructured 2026-07-12.

## The method

The randomization function was a five-faced die — **a physical law, a dead art movement, a browser
feature nobody loves, an institution, a feeling with no name** — rolled fifty times, keeping the
collisions. Constraints honored:

1. Everything is buildable by a coding agent in vanilla Canvas/WebGL/Web Audio/DOM/SVG, self-contained in its own world folder, no CDN.
2. Effort is deliberately spread: some worlds are an evening, some are flagship-scale.
3. Emergent and random behavior is favored over scripted behavior wherever possible.
4. Every idea is left unfinished at the edges on purpose — James's input is expected to polish, redirect, or mutate each one.

**Effort scale:** *an evening* (small, one sitting) / *a weekend* (a real build) / *a Jerry* (flagship-scale, like Jerry's Pool).

**Statuses:** `idea` → `selected` → `building` → `live` → (`retired` if it happens).

## Claude's ideas — the fifty (1–50)

### 1. The Screen Door — *a Jerry* — `idea`
A summer porch at night. One bare bulb, moths orbiting it with real attraction physics, wind chimes on verlet strings, cicadas in synthesized chorus. Pull the light cord: darkness, the moths disperse, the crickets get brave, and something else becomes visible in the yard.

### 2. Fernfrost — *a weekend* — `idea`
A cold windowpane. Frost grows across it in real time via diffusion-limited aggregation — actual fern-crystal math, never the same twice. Your cursor is warm breath that melts it back. You and winter, negotiating for the same glass.

### 3. The Marbling House — *a weekend* — `idea`
A tray of water in a paper-marbling workshop. Drop inks, drag combs and styluses through them using the actual closed-form marbling transforms — the swirls are mathematically perfect and endlessly surprising. When you love one, you pull a print, and it hangs on the workshop wall.

### 4. Stillwater — *a weekend* — `idea`
A pond at dawn reflecting a painted scene in perfect mirror. Touch the water and a true wave-equation ripple shatters the reflection into moving glass. The world is about waiting for it to become whole again.

### 5. Spring Tide — *a weekend* — `idea`
A moonlit beach where the tide height follows the actual current phase of the moon — visit at new moon and the water is hungry. You can pile up sand. The ocean's opinion of your architecture is the whole piece.

### 6. The Fifteen Sisters — *an evening* — `built 2026-07-12` → `src/worlds/the-fifteen-sisters/`
Fifteen pendulums, each a hair shorter than the last, released together. They drift out of phase into snakes, braids, chaos, then — impossibly — snap back into unison. Each one hums a soft tone as it passes center. Hypnosis with a period of sixty seconds.

### 7. The Singing Plate — *a weekend* — `building since 2026-07-10` → `src/worlds/singing-plate/`
A brass plate covered in sand. Drag a violin bow (your cursor) along its edge and it sings; the sand leaps and organizes itself into Chladni resonance patterns — real standing-wave math, geometry summoned out of noise by pitch alone. Change the frequency, the mandala rearranges.

### 8. MORPHOGENESIS DEPT. — *a weekend* — `selected 2026-07-10`
A government-beige laboratory page with one petri dish. Drop two chemicals; Turing reaction-diffusion does the rest — leopard spots, coral brains, fingerprints, all emergent, none designed. Official signage explains nothing. Clipboard available.

### 9. The Pilgrim — *an evening* — `idea`
A monastery courtyard, one monk sweeping in a pattern that looks like madness — he is Langton's ant, executing his two sacred rules. Stay ten thousand steps and the chaos resolves into a perfect road leading off the edge of the world. Enlightenment is real and it is a highway.

### 10. The Underground Post — *a weekend* — `idea`
The forest's mail system, viewed from below. Slime-mold agents (thousands of them, laying and following pheromone trails) grow a living postal network between tree roots, finding routes no one drew. Letters ride the mycelium. Distant cousin to the Dead Letter Office, several floors down.

### 11. Murmur — *a weekend* — `idea`
A power line at dusk, and then ten thousand starlings. Boids flocking with a predator that is sometimes your cursor and sometimes not there at all. The flock is the brushstroke. You will stay longer than you intend.

### 12. Ten Thousand Lanterns — *an evening* — `idea`
A dark riverbank of fireflies, each blinking to its own clock — until, by Kuramoto coupling, they begin to synchronize, patch by patch, into one enormous shared pulse. Click anywhere to scatter the rhythm and watch consensus rebuild itself. Emergence you can vandalize.

### 13. Dewline — *a weekend* — `idea`
An orb-weaver's web at dawn, strung with dew, built strand-by-strand by the actual web-building algorithm while you watch. Pluck a strand and it sounds — the web is a harp tuned by its own tension physics. The spider tolerates your concert. A quiet cousin to Arachno Wars.

### 14. The Delinquent Orrery — *a weekend* — `idea`
A brass Victorian solar system ticking along in perfect Keplerian order. But the planets can be grabbed. Fling Jupiter and honest n-body gravity takes over: moons defect, orbits decay, Mercury leaves. A velvet rope and a small plaque beg you not to.

### 15. How to Retire as Light — *a weekend* — `idea`
Choose a mass. Live one star's ten-billion-year life in four minutes: ignition, the long calm, the red bloat, and then — depending on your choices at birth — a gentle white dwarf or a supernova that seeds the next visitor's sky. An actuarial pamphlet for stars.

### 16. The Dish — *a weekend* — `selected 2026-07-10`
A radio observatory at 3 AM. Steer the antenna across the sky and listen: the Crab pulsar's 33-millisecond purr, Vela's slower heartbeat, the hydrogen line's hiss — real periods, real coordinates — and one unlisted frequency where a calm voice is counting in a language you almost know.

### 17. Aperture — *an evening* — `idea`
A bare concrete room with one rectangular hole in the ceiling, Turrell-style. Through it: the sky as it actually is right now at your location — computed solar position, real dusk, real dawn, real 2 AM black. The page has no other content. It doesn't need any.

### 18. The Nocturne — *a weekend* — `idea`
A velvet-rope lounge that is only open between 2 and 4 AM local time. During the day the page is a shuttered door and a card with the hours. Inside: slow piano, low light, and things worth staying up for.

### 19. TODAY — *an evening* — `idea`

### 20. The Horological Society of Elsewhere — *a weekend* — `idea`

### 21. Take One — *an evening* — `idea`

### 23. The Patience Tree — *a weekend* — `idea`

### 28. The Font Mausoleum — *a weekend* — `idea`

### 30. The Lexicon of Almosts — *a weekend, mostly writing* — `idea`
A dictionary of words that don't exist for feelings that do: the grief of a deleted draft, the intimacy of a shared armrest, the specific loneliness of the last person awake. Full entries — pronunciation, etymology, usage. One word per page, browsed like a real reference.

### 32. Sublevel ℵ — *a weekend* — `idea`
The Library of Babel's basement. Every shelf address deterministically generates its book from a seeded PRNG — which means every book already exists, every page is revisitable, and a URL is a permanent address for one specific page of noise. Somewhere down there is this sentence.

### 33. The Atlas of Elsewhere — *a weekend* — `idea`
Each real-world day, one invisible city — generated from an authored grammar of districts, customs, and weathers, seeded by the date, so every visitor that day walks the same impossible town. Yesterday's city is gone. Calvino as a daily paper.

## Build seeds for the first three

### 7. The Singing Plate
1. Chladni displacement field is closed-form: for plate mode (m, n), amplitude ∝ |cos(mπx)·cos(nπy) − cos(nπx)·cos(mπy)| (square plate). No simulation needed — evaluate the field, done.
2. Sand: 20–40k particles that gradient-descend toward nodal lines (low-amplitude regions) with jitter proportional to local amplitude. This alone produces the "leaping sand" magic.
3. Bowing: drag along the plate's edge; position maps to a (m, n) mode pair, with crossfade morphing between neighboring modes so patterns visibly reorganize.
4. Audio: bowed tone = filtered sawtooth + resonant bandpass partials at the mode frequency; sand hiss = noise keyed to aggregate particle kinetic energy. Gated behind first interaction per site convention.
5. Exit idea to explore: a specific resonance assembles the drift portal's glyph out of sand.

### 8. MORPHOGENESIS DEPT.
1. Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion on a ~256×256 typed-array grid, 2–4 sim steps per frame, palette-mapped and upscaled to the dish. Well-understood, cheap, endlessly generative.
2. "Chemicals" the visitor drops are feed/kill-rate presets: spots, stripes, waves, solitons, worms. Different presets in different dish regions fight at the boundaries — that's the show.
3. The frame is the art direction: government-beige DOM chrome, deadpan signage, an official form, a clipboard. The sim is the easy half; the institutional voice is the co-writing half.
4. Possible mutation later: specimens can be "filed," creating an archive drawer of past cultures (localStorage).

### 16. The Dish
1. Full-screen night sky; the dish silhouette slews in alt-az as you drag. Beam gain = gaussian falloff on angular distance to each catalogued signal.
2. Signal catalog is a small authored JSON: real pulsars at real periods (Crab ~33.4 ms, Vela ~89 ms, PSR B1919+21 ~1.337 s), the hydrogen line as filtered hiss, carrier whistles between stations — plus fictional entries. James can add stations by editing the catalog.
3. Audio: pulse trains through bandpass + noise beds, all Web Audio, gated behind "powering on" the receiver.
4. The unlisted counting voice uses speechSynthesis (site precedent: Wildflowers' Whitman).
5. Possible mutations: a logbook, a signal that only broadcasts at certain real-world hours, coordinates traded between visitors out-of-band.

## Session notes — 2026-07-10

1. Worlds live at time of writing: Jerry's Pool, Wildflowers at Dusk (landscape-plate work in progress, uncommitted), The Dead Letter Office (draft), The Monochrome Rift (renamed The Chrome Rift 2026-07-15), Pelagic Lantern Habitat.

3. 5. Registers deliberately covered across the fifty: nature/physics emergence, durational/conceptual time pieces, typography and prose, games and places, sound instruments, and browser-as-haunted-house. If a build stalls, swap registers rather than pushing — the list is wide on purpose.
