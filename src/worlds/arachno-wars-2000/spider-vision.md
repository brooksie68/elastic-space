# The Spider Vision — James's north star (2026-07-15)

James's long-range vision for the spider tanks, recorded from his own words and his three
Procreate drawings in `assets/reference/` (Spider_Tank_1/2/3.png). **Arachno-Wars 2000 is
not this game — but every round of work on it should be a stepping stone toward this.**

## The machines

- Circa 2500. Highly autonomous, highly intelligent, very deadly.
- Built of an **intelligent carbon metal** that the AI inside the tank forms and deforms
  at will, by thought.
- Low-slung and wide, tarantula stance, evil-looking, black. Small on screen but
  detail-dense — deadly machines read from a distance back.
- **Leg motion language** (the key to everything): creepy and whip-like. A leg moves
  slowly through its arc, then the tip *whips* out and grabs on. Tips are needle-pointed;
  variants carry claws and grippers. Reference: Spider_Tank_2.png has the definitive
  silhouette — needle legs, high whip kinks, minimal body.
- Because the material is thought-formed, legs and hull can become **tools**: hundreds of
  options — weapons, grippers, defensive forms, jumping, rocket boost (Spider_Tank_2
  shows the exhaust), tunneling, etc. Combos of options are the strategy space.

## The game (someday)

- 2D side-scroller. Each level is a mission: start at the left, work right through
  enemies and obstacles, take out a final level boss.
- Levels are terrain puzzles as much as fights: overhangs, C-shaped rock croppings,
  crevices, cliffs (Spider_Tank_3.png) — climb up, down, around; surfaces in any
  orientation; height advantage duels with beam weapons.
- Hundreds of capability options and combos; many creative ways to overcome each
  level's challenges. Player expression through loadout and improvisation.

## Stepping stones inside Arachno-Wars 2000

Ordered by how directly they serve the north star:

1. **Whip-leg motion language** (roadmap #19) — replace the sinusoid leg gait with
   slow-reach + tip-whip procedural animation, needle tips. Pure presentation, buildable
   in this game now, and it IS the soul of the machines.
2. **Per-part black carbon re-render** (roadmap #10) — the look.
3. **Rocket boost** — as a mobility card in this game (turn-limited hop with that
   red-orange exhaust). Folds into roadmap #16's "new weapons/options" territory.
   — `shipped 2026-07-15`: SHIFT = fuel-metered rocket thrust with the Spider_Tank_2
   exhaust plume, plus auto retro-burst + leg-catch on hard landings. W is pure jump
   (double-tap = one bigger boost).
4. **Climbing arenas** (roadmap #18) — polygon terrain + surface-normal walking is the
   side-scroller's traversal core, prototyped here.
5. **Beam-duel emphasis** — beams as the positional weapon (already in; keep leaning in).
6. **Tunneling** — the beam already melts rock; a dig mode is its cousin. Unscheduled.

When most of these exist, the side-scroller stops being a dream and becomes a port.
