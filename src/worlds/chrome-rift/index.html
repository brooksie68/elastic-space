    The Chrome Rift tuner

    1. speed — the full-width slider (about half your screen). Runs from a dead stop on the left to ×800 on the right, where the bands smear into a gray blur. The scale is position⁴, so the left half of the track covers roughly ×0–×30 for fine adjustment and the violence lives at the far right. The readout shows the current multiplier ("stopped" at zero). High speeds render as true motion blur — sub-frame instants averaged together — never strobing.
    2. bands — how many bands split the rift, 3 to 60 (default 18). Bands come from a fixed pool of 60 personalities, so sliding down and back up returns the same bands you had. Width scales with count: three colossal slabs at the low end, dense slivers at the high end.
    3. wander — how far each band roams from its home position, 0–100% (default 6%). Squared mapping keeps the left half subtle; at 100% bands sweep the entire screen width and cross each other. At 0% they stand at attention and only shimmer in place.
    4. breathe — how hard the rift breathes with the music, 0–100% (default 50%). An analyser listens to the audio and auto-ranges its level, so even a steady track produces a usable breath. Band speed swells up to ~×1.9 and the center glow grows and brightens on the inhales. Needs sound on; does nothing at 0% or when muted.
    5. pull — how strongly the center glow follows your cursor, 0–100% (default 50%). The glow eases toward the pointer over about a second; the percentage blends its target between its home spot (62% across, centered vertically) and your cursor. It drifts home when the cursor leaves the window.
    6. edges — clarity of the band edges, 0–100% (default 50% = the original look). Left of center applies an increasing fog blur over the whole band field; right of center sharpens the gradient transitions until 100% is hard-edged solid slabs.
    7. gradient — two color pickers for the start and end colors of the band gradient (default white → black). Everything derives from the palette: bands, scanlines, glow tint, and the backdrop (built from the deepest color).
    8. presets — 13 chips next to the pickers, each showing its own mini-gradient.
    9. reset — the pill at the end of the colors row. One click returns all seven parameters and the palette to defaults. Individually, double-clicking any slider resets just that one.
